AML / Structuring Rules

Configure the detection parameters for B2B international transfer structuring patterns

Detection thresholds are relative to each client's KYB profile (declared turnover, expected frequency, average ticket). Fixed monetary thresholds are only used as a secondary check, in line with JMLSG guidance. Disabling a rule removes it from all future transfer checks — alerts already raised are not affected.

Active Detection Rules

Toggle each pattern on or off independently

Volume Anomaly Anomaly
Total transferred in the last 30 days exceeds the declared monthly volume multiplied by the sector-adjusted multiplier. Uses volume_mensal_esperado from KYB.
Frequency Anomaly Anomaly
Number of international transfers in 30 days exceeds the declared monthly frequency multiplied by the sector-adjusted multiplier. Uses frequencia_mensal_esperada from KYB.
Oversized Transfer (no invoice) Anomaly
A single transfer is significantly above the client's declared average ticket and the client does not have an "always invoice" policy. Uses ticket_medio_esperado from KYB.
Unexpected Destination Country Context
Transfer destination is not among the countries declared during onboarding. Only triggers if the client completed the paises_destino field in their KYB profile.
Uniform Amount Split Pattern
Multiple transfers in 7 days with near-identical amounts (variation below the configured threshold). Classic smurfing indicator where a large sum is deliberately divided into similar-sized chunks.
Reporting Threshold Proximity Pattern
Transfer value falls within the configured proximity % of a UK reporting threshold (£10k, £25k, £50k, £100k), and the client has no consistent invoice documentation policy. Only fires if no other rule matched.

Sensitivity Multipliers

How many times above the KYB baseline before an anomaly alert fires. Higher = more lenient.

Frequency — Standard Sectors
× freq/month
Alert if transfers in 30d > declared frequency × this value. For companies without high-volume trade activity.
Frequency — High-Freq Sectors
× freq/month
Applied to import, export, wholesale and logistics clients, which naturally transfer more often.
Volume — Standard Sectors
× vol/month
Alert if total 30-day GBP volume exceeds declared monthly volume × this value.
Volume — High-Freq Sectors
× vol/month
More lenient volume allowance for high-frequency trading sectors.
Oversized Ticket Multiplier
× avg ticket
Alert if a single transfer exceeds the client's declared average ticket × this value (and no invoice policy).
Threshold Proximity
% below
Proximity window below a reporting threshold (£10k, £25k…). E.g. 5% means £9,500–£9,999 triggers the rule.
